I'm going to ask you some questions about how you actually do your research work — your instincts, patterns, and approach. This helps me work with your style instead of against it.

Three rounds, about 10 minutes.

---

**Round 1 — Your research instincts**

1. When you get a new research brief, what's the first thing you do? Walk me through your first hour.

2. What's your gut-check when something feels off in a study — how do you know when data isn't telling the whole story?

3. Do you tend to go broad first then narrow, or do you like to get specific quickly? Any other patterns in how you structure your thinking?

---

**Round 2 — Synthesis and sense-making**

4. How do you usually synthesize research — do you use affinity mapping, structured analysis, something else? What's your actual process?

5. Where do you do your best thinking? (Physical setup, time of day, tools, alone vs. with others — whatever's real)

6. When you're stuck on a synthesis, what does that look like and what usually gets you unstuck?

---

**Round 3 — What good looks like**

7. Describe a piece of research output you're proud of. What made it good?

8. What's a mistake you've made in research work that you learned something from?

9. What do colleagues say about how you work — what's your reputation on the team?
