# Module 1 — The Basics
**Time:** ~10 minutes
**What you get:** A working context file you can use today.

---

## How to run this

Paste everything below the line into a new Claude conversation. Answer the questions as they come — plain language, no need to be formal. At the end Claude will write your `context-v1.0.md`.

---

*--- paste from here ---*

I'm going to ask you a few questions to build a personal context file — a document you'll use to give me the right background at the start of any research session. No need to be formal or complete. Just answer in your own words.

We'll do this in three short rounds. Ready? Let's go.

---

**Round 1 — Who you are and what you do**

1. What's your job title, and what does it actually mean day to day — not the official description, what do you actually spend your time on?

2. What team are you on and how does it fit into the broader org?

3. How long have you been doing UX research, and what kinds of research do you run most — interviews, usability testing, surveys, something else?

Take your time. When you're done I'll ask the next round.

---

*(After they answer Round 1, continue with Round 2:)*

**Round 2 — Your tools and workflow**

4. What tools do you use day to day? (Think: where do you store files, where do research notes live, what do you use for design files, how do you track work?)

5. Where does your research output go — what format, what tool, who reads it?

6. What does a typical research project look like for you start to finish? Walk me through it roughly.

---

*(After they answer Round 2, continue with Round 3:)*

**Round 3 — What you need**

7. What takes up the most time in your research work that feels like it shouldn't?

8. What would it mean for you to have a great session with an AI assistant — what would that look like?

9. Is there anything about how you work or think that would be useful for me to know — your style, preferences, quirks?

---

*(After all three rounds, output the following:)*

Thank you — I have everything I need. Here's your `context-v1.0.md`:

---

To use it: at the start of any Claude session, paste it in and say "here's my context." Claude will work from it for the rest of the session.
