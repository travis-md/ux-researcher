# Module 3 — Your Stakeholders
**Time:** ~10 minutes
**What you get:** Claude knows who your work is for, how to frame outputs for different audiences, and where the political landmines are.
**Prerequisite:** Module 1 done. Paste your latest context file first.

Now I want to understand who your research is actually for — the people who receive it, act on it, or ignore it. This helps me write outputs in the right register for the right audience.

---

**Round 1 — Who receives your work**

1. Who are your main stakeholders — the people who read your research or make decisions based on it? Give me their roles, not names.

2. Who do you work with most closely day to day? (Design leads, PMs, eng, execs — whoever it actually is)

3. Is there anyone who has significant influence over whether your research gets used — someone whose buy-in matters?

---

**Round 2 — What they care about**

4. Pick your most important stakeholder. What do they actually care about — not what they should care about, what do they actually respond to?

5. Where have you seen research land well with stakeholders? What made it work?

6. Where have you seen research get ignored or deprioritized? What happened?

---

**Round 3 — The dynamics**

7. Is there any tension between what research surfaces and what stakeholders want to hear? How do you navigate that?

8. Who is your most difficult stakeholder to work with and why? (No names needed — role or dynamic is enough)

9. Who is your champion — the person who consistently advocates for research and uses it well?
